Dissection of what makes faceless soccer content go viral, pulled from real top accounts and short-form retention data. The goal: **steal the formula, rebuild it in our copyright-safe lane** (stats/spotlights/original VO), under the TikiTakaFootyTV brand. These patterns become rules in the `BRAND_SYSTEM` prompt + a library of format templates.

## 2. Format archetypes that go viral (with evidence)

1. **Player edits / comparison edits** — Vinicius, Neymar, Szoboszlai edits hitting **7M+ views**. (Footage-heavy → we adapt as *stat-driven* spotlights, not broadcast clips.)
2. **Trend-flipping** — take a trending comedy/fitness format and reskin it as football. Highest-leverage move: you ride an existing wave instead of starting one.
3. **Skill recreation / tutorials** — recreating famous plays (Declan Rice freekick, **2.3M+**), juggling/technique tutorials.
4. **Stats & graphics** — 433/90 Min's whole engine. Match info, "this player's season in numbers," head-to-heads. **This is our core copyright-safe lane.**
5. **Q&A / narrative videos** — BR's question-answer storytelling format.
6. **Memes / banter / debate** — FTBL, Rivalsbanter. Engineered for comment wars.
7. **AI cinematic stadium content** — emerging; AI-generated atmosphere/crowd visuals (also copyright-safe B-roll for us).

## 3. The cross-account formula (the patterns that repeat)

### A. The hook (first 1–3 seconds) — the whole game
- Target a **3-second hold rate ≥ 60%** (top content hits 70–80%). Everything else is irrelevant if the hook fails.
- Three proven hook engines: **curiosity/information gap**, **visual interrupt** (because most watch **sound-off** — the *first frame's text* must hook), and **direct promise** ("the stat that broke the Prem").
- **First subtitle ≤ 7 words.** **No dissolve transitions in the first 5 seconds** — hard cuts only.

### B. Retention mechanics
- **Open loops / withheld payoff** — "#1 will surprise you," countdowns where the best lands last.
- **Seamless loop** — end frame flows back into the first to farm re-watches (boosts completion %).
- Fast pacing, a clear payoff, and a reason to stay to the end.

### C. Comment-engineering (the algorithm's rocket fuel)
- **Comment-keyword CTAs drive ~1.5–2.2× comments.** Ask a question with a *wrong answer people can't resist correcting*.
- **Engineer disagreement**: rankings with a "disrespectful" omission, this-or-that, hot takes. Rivalsbanter's entire model.
- **"Save" CTAs lift reach over 7–14 days** — stats/"did you know" posts are inherently save-able.

### D. Speed + cadence + iteration (433's real secret)
- **Keep it short** — 433's early golden rule was **under 15 seconds**.
- **Ship fast, kill fast** — idea in the morning, posted within 2 hours, replaced by afternoon if it flopped.
- **Track engagement by the second** and let data pick winners.
- **A/B the hook** — make 2–3 versions of a video with different opening hooks; keep what holds.

## 4. The dissection rubric — filled in

| Dimension | The winning pattern |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Surprising single stat / bold claim / question, as **on-screen text** by frame 1; ≤7 words |
| **Format archetype** | Stat reveal, ranking/countdown, this-or-that, "did you know," season-in-numbers, glow-up |
| **Emotional trigger** | Tribalism, awe, nostalgia, debate, "I'm smart for knowing this" |
| **Retention mechanic** | Open loop ("#1 shocked me") + countdown + seamless loop |
| **Length & pacing** | Short (≤15–30s), hard cuts, payoff withheld to the end |
| **On-screen text** | Bold, big, ≤7-word first line, no dissolves early |
| **Audio** | Original VO (our lane) over a music bed; ride trending sounds when safe |
| **Caption + hook line** | A question that invites a *correction* or *side-pick* |
| **Comment driver** | Disagreement by design — omission, ranking, this-or-that, "name a better…" |

## 5. Translation to TikiTakaFootyTV (copyright-safe rebuilds)

The key move: **separate the viral mechanic from the footage.** A "Top 5 worldies" reel works because of *countdown + payoff + debate*, not because it's broadcast video. Rebuild the mechanic as graphics/VO:

| Viral format (theirs) | Our copyright-safe rebuild |
|---|---|
| Top-5 goals reel (broadcast clips) | **Top-5 stat countdown** — animated graphics, VO, "#1 will shock you" |
| Player edit (highlight footage) | **Player season-in-numbers spotlight** — animated stat cards + VO |
| Banter/ranking debate | **"Rank these 5" / this-or-that stat graphic** — engineered for comment wars |
| "Did you know" over clips | **Did-you-know stat card** — pure graphic + VO, highly *save-able* |
| Trend-flip with footage | **Trend-flip with a stat reveal** as the punchline |

## 6. Starter templates to run (week 1)

1. **The Shock Stat** — open on one surprising number (on-screen, ≤7 words), VO explains in 15s. Caption asks a debate question. *(save-bait + comment-bait)*
2. **Top-5 Countdown** — stat-ranked, #1 withheld, seamless loop. CTA: "did I get #1 wrong?"
3. **This-or-That** — two players, one stat, "pick one 👇." Pure comment engine.
4. **Season in Numbers** — one player, 4–5 animated stat cards, hype VO. Spotlight + save-bait.
5. **Disrespectful Ranking** — deliberately omit a fan-favorite from a top list. Engineered outrage. Use sparingly.
6. **The Top-5 Quiz** ⭐ *(added from the outlier engine — Tifo's highest-overperforming format, 4–5× baseline)* — "Can you name [entity]'s top 5 [stat]?" Question hook → "pause and guess" board → reveal one by one → "how many did you get? 👇". Pure names+stats, copyright-safe, elite comment + save bait. **Accuracy-critical** — ground the list via the KB.

Every one of these is: copyright-safe, fully scriptable by Claude into the `VideoSpec` schema, and built on a proven viral mechanic. Run all five, **track by the second, kill the losers, A/B the hooks** — that's the 433 method applied to our lane.
